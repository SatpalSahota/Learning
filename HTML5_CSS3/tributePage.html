Aneurin Bevan

The Father of the NHS

Aneurin Bevan - Offical MP Portrait

  • 1897 - Born on 15th November in Tredegar, Monmouthshire, Wales to a coal miner and a seamstress, one of ten children.
  • 1900 - Leaves school at the age of thirteen, having repeated a year due to poor perfomance, and starts working at Ty-Trist colliery.
  • 1919 - Attends the Central Labour College in London, having won a scholarship to attend (sponsored by the South Wales Miner's Federation).
  • 1920/1921 - Helps found the Tredegar Query Club, which aids those in the club who need financial aid to weaken the stronghold the mining companies had on the area.
  • 1926 - Starts working as a union member official. Helped to head the General Strike of 1926 in the South Wales mining community.
  • 1928 - Wins a seat on Monmouthshire City Council, shortly followed by winning the nomination for the parliamentary seat of Ebbw Vale, and holds the seat in the 1929 and 1931 general elections.
  • 1934 - Marries Jennie Lee.
  • 1937 - Speaks out against rearmament in response to the rise Adolf Hitler's Germany:

    "If the immediate international situation is used as an excuse to get us to drop our opposition to the rearmament programme of the Government, the next phase must be that we must desist from any industrial or political action that may disturb national unity in the face of Fascist aggression. Along that road is endless retreat, and at the end of it a voluntary totalitarian State with ourselves erecting the barbed wire around. You cannot collaborate, you cannot accept the logic of collaboration on a first class issue like rearmament, and at the same time evade the implications of collaboration all along the line when the occasion demands it."

  • 1945 - Labour, under Clement Attlee, win the General Election and come into power, with Attlee appointing Bevan as Minister for Health. He begins to work on a new, universal, nationalised health service (the NHS).
  • 1946 - Successfully passes the National Health Service Act 1946, paving the way for the introduction of the NHS.
  • 1948 - On 5th July 1948, the National Health Service Act comes into force, and his vision for a universal healthcare system becomes a reality.
  • 1951 - Is appointed Minister of Labour, but resigns soon after after Hugh Gaitskell's introduction of prescription charges to partly fund the Korean War effort.
  • 1951 - Labour lose that year's General Election.
  • 1955 - Contests the leadership of the Labour Party, but loses out to Hugh Gaitskell. Becomes Shadow Foreign Seceretary the following year.
  • 1959 - Bevan is elected Deputy Leader of the Labour Party. Complaining of an ulcer, he undergoes surgery to find he has malignant stomach cancer. Gives his final speech to the House of Commons on 3rd December.
  • 1960 - dies at 16:10 on 6th July, aged 62. His remains were cremated at Gwent Crematorium in Croesyceiliog.

Aneurin Bevan, In Place of Fear, p. 100
